Setup guide

Welcome — and thank you for helping test caidal. This takes about 10 minutes, and no tech skills are needed. If you can install an app, you can do this.

You'll need an iPhone (or iPad) on a recent version of iOS. If anything won't install, it's usually because the phone needs a quick update: Settings → General → Software Update.

1 · Get the app (three steps)

caidal isn't in the App Store yet, so we use Apple's official tool for trying apps early, called TestFlight. It's made by Apple and completely safe.

  • Install TestFlight — open the App Store, search “TestFlight,” and tap Get (it's free).
  • Accept your invitation — open the “You're invited to test caidal” email on your iPhone and tap “View in TestFlight,” then Accept.
  • Install caidal — tap Install. That's it — caidal is now on your home screen.

Because it's a test version, caidal may update often. When it does, TestFlight will let you know — just tap Update.

2 · Your first time opening caidal

  • You can skip anything. If a screen feels like too much, look for “Skip and revisit in app” — you can always fill things in later.
  • Setting up your Profile is optional but helpful — the more caidal knows, the more personalized its guidance. Share only what you're comfortable with.
  • Take your time. Nothing is permanent, and you can change everything later.

3 · Apple Health (totally optional)

caidal can connect to Apple Health to read your clinical records so its answers are more tailored to you. This is optional — the app works fully without it. If you'd rather not, just skip that step.

  • If you'd like to try it, tap “Turn On All” (or pick items) when caidal asks, then Allow.
  • caidal only reads what you approve; you can turn it off anytime in Settings → Privacy & Security → Health.
  • On an iPad, Apple Health isn't available — that's normal; everything else still works.

4 · Is my information safe?

  • Sharing is your choice, item by item — by default your data stays on your phone.
  • What you do share is sent securely to OpenAI only to generate your answers.
  • Your data is not used to train AI models, and not visible to anyone else.
  • You're always in control — review, remove, or delete anything at any time.

5 · How to share your thoughts

This is the whole point — your honest first impressions. Two easy ways:

  • Inside TestFlight: take a screenshot while using caidal, and TestFlight will offer to send it to us with a note.
  • Or just email us anything — what confused you, what you liked, what felt off. Even “I didn't get it” is incredibly useful.

That's everything. Thank you for being early — the first people to believe in something are the ones who make it real. 🙌

Step-by-step testing guide

caidal has a lot in it — so here's a simple, ~15–20 minute path through the parts that matter most. Do as much or as little as you like; even the first couple of steps is a real help.

The one idea that makes it click

Almost everything caidal creates follows the same three-beat rhythm: Generate it → Develop it → Commit to it. You ask caidal to generate a first draft, you develop it (chat to refine, add, reshape until it's right), then you commit to it — and it lands in the matching visit tab, ready to use. After you commit, pop into that tab to see what you locked in. Feel that rhythm once and the whole app makes sense.

Where do you do it? The ⋯ (three dots) menu in the top-right corner is your command center for everything caidal generates.

The other idea: capture beats perfection

caidal gets better the more it knows about you — richer data means more personalized, more accurate answers; too little, and its reliability drops. So capturing is meant to be effortless, and that's what Scribbles are for: Capture it fast → Refine it later → Repurpose it anywhere. Get a rough thought out of your head the moment it hits — jot or speak a symptom, a worry, a measurement — without stopping to make it tidy. Later you shape it and turn it into a Note or a Personal Data point you can attach to any visit. The point: never lose a detail because capturing it felt like work.

Optional first move (one tap)

caidal can pull your real medical records straight from Apple Health with a single button — instant, realistic data to explore with. Totally optional: if you'd rather not, or you have no records there, that's completely fine — nothing is required, and skipping it won't break or block anything.

If you do pull them: your records stay on your device, and caidal only reads what you approve (read-only). Nothing is ever sent anywhere unless you personally choose to share a specific item with the assistant.

Before you dive in

The app comes preloaded with example visits and data, so you're never staring at a blank screen. You're welcome to explore all of that — but do it before or after this walkthrough, not during. While you're on the steps, just build your own case so nothing pulls your focus.

Pick a “case”

Think of a health thing you've dealt with — a trick knee, migraines, blood pressure, a recent check-up. Nothing comes to mind? Make one up (say, recurring headaches). That's your storyline for the next few minutes.

1 · Prepare for a visit

  • Create a new visit for your case (e.g. “Headaches — primary care”).
  • Open the visit's chat and just talk to it — what's going on, what worries you, what you'd want to ask.
  • Now the rhythm: tap the ⋯ menu (top-right) → Generate Visit Prep → develop it (chat to refine, add a question, reshape until it feels like your briefing) → commit to it. (You'll use that same ⋯ menu to generate the Alerts and Actions later, so get comfortable with it now.)
  • Head to the Pre-Visit tab and open your committed Visit Prep — that's the document you'd actually keep open as your guide during the appointment. (The pattern everywhere: once you commit, check the matching tab to see what you locked in.)

2 · Give it context

  • Add related data to the visit — attach an existing medical record (if you imported from Apple Health), or add a bit of personal data (a measurement, a symptom). Old or rough is fine; we just want the flow.

3 · “Attend” the visit

  • In the In-Visit tab, hit record and talk for a bit — say some doctor-visit-sounding things out loud. Stop, and check the transcription.
  • Same rhythm, same place: from the ⋯ menu (top-right), generate the Alerts (things worth a closer look) → develop → commit → then check the Alerts tab to see what you kept.
  • In the Post-Visit tab, upload a discharge / after-visit document for your case (an old real one is perfect), then from the ⋯ menu again, generate the Actions (and Alerts) → develop → commit → go see them in the tab.

4 · Try Scribbles (quick capture)

  • Create a text scribble about your case → convert it into a Note.
  • Create an audio scribble — just speak out a health observation: a symptom, a measurement, a feeling, anything that sounds like a personal health data point (e.g. “Slept badly again, morning headache about a 6 out of 10”) → convert it into Personal Data.
  • Add both to your visit.

5 · See the part we're proudest of

  • Open the preloaded ankle-sprain visits — three visits for the same problem over time. Open the latest and notice it already knows about the earlier ones. That “it remembers, so you don't have to” thread is the heart of the whole app.

When you're done

Roam freely — explore the example visits, dig through the ⋯ menu, break things. Then tell us what you think: screenshot anytime inside TestFlight (it'll offer to send it to us with a note), or just email us. What confused you, what you liked, what felt slow or pointless — brutal honesty is a gift. 🙌
